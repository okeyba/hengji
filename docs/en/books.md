# Books and split-accounting: keep business money and personal money apart

The core idea of Hengji is to let you keep your "household account" and your "shop account" in one piece of software at the same time, without mixing the two into one muddy pot.

**Who it's for**: street vendors, small-shop owners, tiny online sellers, and community group-buy organizers — sole proprietors and small bosses, especially anyone whose one Alipay account both pays for inventory and buys groceries, leaving business money and personal money tangled together for the long haul.

## The one-sentence version

- A **Book** is a self-contained set of accounts. One for home, one for the shop, neither interfering with the other.
- **Real-world wallets** (cash, bank card, Alipay, WeChat) can be marked as "globally shared" and used across multiple Books — because in reality they really are one and the same account.

It's the same money, but "does this belong to the business or to daily life" stays crystal clear. Filing taxes and working out how much the shop actually made no longer means staring at a pile of jumbled transactions.

## Three kinds of Book

When you create a Book you pick a type, and Hengji sets you up with a ready-to-use set of accounts (income/expense categories) that match that type:

| Type | Who it's for | Roughly what's inside |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 👤 Personal | Tracking household / personal day-to-day income and spending | Cash, bank card, Alipay, WeChat wallet, credit card, Huabei; salary, dining, transport, shopping, housing, etc. |
| 💼 Business | Tracking shop / business operations | Corporate account, WeChat Merchant, Alipay Merchant, cash, accounts receivable; operating revenue, cost of goods, shipping and sundry fees, etc. |
| 📈 Investment | Tracking a single investment on its own | Investment account, investment gains/losses, opening balance |

> **The Investment Book is hidden by default.** It's an advanced feature: you first have to go to [Settings](settings.md) and "Enable merchant advanced features" before the "Investment" option shows up when creating a Book. For everyday use, the "Personal" and "Business" types are all most users need.

You can create more than one Book of the same type. For example, if a couple each keeps their own records, create two "Personal" Books ("My Daily" and "Wife's Book"); if you run two shops, create two "Business" Books.

## Creating, switching, and archiving Books

All Books live in the "Books" section of the left sidebar.

**Create a new Book:**

1. Click "＋ New Book" in the left sidebar.
2. Enter a Book name (e.g. "My Little Shop").
3. Choose a type: Personal / Business (Investment only appears once advanced features are enabled).
4. Click "Create". Hengji automatically sets up the default accounts for that type of Book and takes you straight into the new Book.

**Switch Books:** just click a Book name in the left sidebar. The current Book is highlighted, and the main view switches with it.

**Archive a Book:** open the Book you want to archive, click "Archive Book" in the top-right corner, and confirm. After archiving:

- The Book is hidden from the left sidebar list, and the [Global overview](#global-overview-every-book-on-one-screen) no longer counts it in.
- **No data is deleted** — it's simply tucked away.
- Your shared wallets (globally-shared accounts) are kept even if they were created inside the archived Book; they don't disappear from your other Books.

## The hero scenario: one wallet, the money split across two Books

This is the single most important pain point Hengji solves for sole proprietors.

Suppose you have one Alipay account that both pays the shop's inventory bills and buys the family's groceries. In the past you'd either mix business and personal records together and never sort them out, or you'd artificially split them into two wallets — which feels awkward.

Hengji's approach: **mark this Alipay account as a "globally shared" account**, and then —

- in the "My Daily" Book, log groceries and rent;
- in the "My Little Shop" Book, log inventory payments and money received from customers;
- both Books can pick the same Alipay account to post against.

So every entry is clearly attributed to "business" or "daily life," yet the wallet balance is always the real balance of this one real Alipay account — no manual reconciliation needed. Once an account is set as shared, a small "Global" tag appears next to its name in the left sidebar.

For how to mark an account as globally shared and which accounts qualify (only genuine money wallets can be shared; "virtual accounts" like accounts receivable or inventory cannot), see [Accounts](accounts.md).

### How it works (just the essentials)

Under the hood Hengji is rigorous double-entry bookkeeping, but all you do is "log an entry" — the debit/credit lines are generated by the software automatically. Here you only need to know one thing:

- **The balance of a real wallet (cash / bank / Alipay / WeChat) belongs to "global funds"** and is not counted into the "net worth" of any single Book — because this money is shared across several Books, and counting it into any one of them would double-count it.
- **What each Book computes on its own is the "operating / living" portion**: how much you earned and spent this month, how much A/R and inventory the shop holds.

So on the [Global overview](#global-overview-every-book-on-one-screen) you'll see "global funds" listed on its own line, while each Book shows only its own dedicated portion — they add up without overlap or conflict.

## Global overview: every Book on one screen

The "🧮 Global overview" at the very top of the left sidebar is the summary entry point for all Books (and the default landing page when you open Hengji). On one screen it gives you:

- **Total net worth**: globally-shared funds plus the sum of each Book's dedicated net amount — your whole household balance across several Books combined, with no double-counting.
- **Total income / total expense / total net this month**: this month's ins and outs across all Books combined.
- **A card per Book**: one card for each Book, showing its net amount and net for the month (Business Books show "operating net" and "profit this month"; Personal Books show "dedicated net" and "net for the month"). Click a card to jump straight into that Book.

If you hold foreign-currency accounts, net worth is also broken out into per-currency subtotals plus a converted grand total. For multi-currency details, see [Accounts](accounts.md).

## Default-account differences across Book types

When you create a Book, Hengji automatically sets up a suite of accounts based on the type, so you don't have to build from scratch:

- **Personal**: pure living income and spending — cash / bank card / Alipay / WeChat wallet / credit card / Huabei, plus categories like salary, dining, transport, shopping, housing, entertainment, and medical. No business elements at all.
- **Business**: operating accounts — corporate account / WeChat Merchant / Alipay Merchant / cash / accounts receivable, plus operating revenue, cost of goods, shipping and sundry fees, and so on. Accounts like inventory and accounts payable are created automatically by the system on demand once you actually start purchasing or buying on credit — you won't be greeted by a pile of accounts you don't understand up front.
- **Investment**: the simplest — investment account, investment gains/losses, opening balance — purpose-built to track the present-value ups and downs of a single investment.

Accounts aren't hard-coded: you can rename, add, and remove them to suit your own habits. For how, see [Accounts](accounts.md).
